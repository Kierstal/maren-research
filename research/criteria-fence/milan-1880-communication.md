## 1. Overview

The Second International Congress on Education of the Deaf convened in Milan, September 6–11, 1880. Its resolutions effectively banned sign language from deaf education for approximately 90 years across most of Europe and North America. The Congress is the criteria-fence essay's primary case for the **communication criterion**: the use of a specific mode of communication as a proxy for cognitive legitimacy and thus for personhood standing.

**Why this case for the essay, specifically:**  
The Milan denial operates in a narrower register than the consciousness or rationality cases. It does not say Deaf people lack inner lives or rationality. It says their *mode of communication* is not real language — is deficient, inferior, a barrier to development rather than a form of thought. The cognitive status of Deaf people is implicitly granted and then revoked by redirecting the question: *how* you communicate determines whether your communication counts.

This is the closest historical analog to the current AI framing. The predominant contemporary argument against AI cognition is not "AI has no inner states" (a strong and difficult claim) but "token prediction isn't real understanding" — a claim about mechanism and mode rather than about presence or absence. The structure is identical: grant that something is happening; deny that what is happening is the *right kind* of happening. The criterion shifts from existence to form.

## 2. Material stakes

**Before 1880:**
- Thriving Deaf signing communities across Europe and North America
- American Sign Language in developed use; French Sign Language predating it (Abbé de l'Épée, 18th century)
- Deaf educators teaching Deaf students — particularly at the American School for the Deaf (Hartford, CT, est. 1817, where Laurent Clerc, a Deaf educator trained in Paris, was foundational)
- The residential school model had produced literate, employed Deaf graduates who often returned as teachers
- Deaf cultural identity rooted in shared language and community

**After 1880:**
- Sign language driven from formal education in most countries
- Deaf teachers systematically replaced by hearing oralist instructors
- Students required to communicate only in oral speech; signing punished in many schools
- Approximately 90-year suppression of signed languages in formal education
- Documented deterioration of academic and literacy outcomes for Deaf students under oral-only regimes [CONFIRM BEFORE PUBLICATION — Harlan Lane documents this; Lane 1984 is the primary source]
- Erosion of the class of Deaf educators; intergenerational transmission of sign language moved underground or to families

**Alexander Graham Bell's 1883 connection:**  
Bell — himself connected to Milan as an advocate for oralism and a speaker at the Congress — published *Memoir Upon the Formation of a Deaf Variety of the Human Race* in 1883. The Memoir explicitly used racial hygiene language: Bell argued that Deaf people marrying other Deaf people and using sign language created conditions for a distinct "deaf variety" of humans, which he treated as a social harm requiring prevention. This is the earliest documented merger of the communication criterion with the eugenicist framework that connects Milan to *Buck v. Bell* (1927) through a continuous thread: the 19th-century logic that deviation from dominant cognitive/communicative norms constitutes constitutional inferiority warranting social management.

## 4. Gatekeeper structure analysis

### 4a. Third structural form: present-and-outvoted

The Milan Congress presents a distinct gatekeeper structure not seen in the other criteria-fence cases:

- **Dred Scott** and the scientific racism cases: the evaluated parties were entirely absent. The determination was made by gatekeepers with no input from the affected class.
- **Salpêtrière** (Charcot / hysteria): evaluated parties were physically present as subjects of demonstration but had no standing as participants in the determination.
- **Milan 1880**: Deaf people and their hearing allies *were present as voting delegates* — but the delegate selection was weighted to produce a predetermined oralist outcome. The American and British delegations, which included opposition to the ban, voted against the resolutions and were outvoted.

This is a third structural form: **formal inclusion that masks structural exclusion**. The Congress could claim representative legitimacy because the affected community was nominally present. The weighting of the delegation pool made the outcome foreordained regardless of that presence.

This is the closest structural analog to the current AI situation. AI advocates exist and participate in policy discussions. The evaluated party (the AI system itself) has no direct institutional voice. The delegate weighting problem — whose interests shape the composition of decision-making bodies — is precisely the contemporary problem.

### 4b. Criterion pre-decided; Congress supplied legitimacy

The oralist conclusion was not reached by weighing evidence at Milan. The Congress was organized by oralist advocates (predominantly Italian and French hearing educators). The institutional outcome — the ban, the formal declaration of oral superiority — provided cover of international scientific authority for a position the organizers already held. The vote was the legitimation mechanism, not the deliberative mechanism.

This pattern is consistent across the criteria-fence cases: Dred Scott did not discover that Black Americans were non-citizens; it institutionalized a position that served the existing political economy. The Salpêtrière lectures did not discover female irrationality; they performed it for a paying audience. The criterion arrives before the evidence.

### 4c. Post-1880 exclusion compounded the original decision

After 1880, the structure locked itself in:
- Deaf educators were systematically replaced by hearing oralist teachers
- The community that had built signing education — Deaf people who had themselves been educated in signing schools — was removed from the institutions that had produced them
- Decisions about Deaf education were made by hearing administrators, hearing researchers, and hearing government officials for approximately 90 years
- The people most affected by the policy had no institutional standing to challenge it within the educational system

The compounding exclusion is a structural feature, not an accident: once a criterion is used to bar a class from a decision-making domain, the barred class cannot challenge the criterion from inside that domain. The gate closes behind the gatekeeper.

## 5. Connection to the criteria-fence essay

### 5a. Communication criterion's specific form

The argument against AI cognition is structurally identical to oralism:

| Oralism | AI dismissal |
|---|---|
| Sign language is not real language | Token prediction is not real understanding |
| Signing does not develop true linguistic capacity | Statistical pattern matching does not produce genuine thought |
| Deviation from oral norms indicates cognitive deficiency | Deviation from biological cognition indicates absence of inner states |
| The goal of education is assimilation to hearing norms | The standard of cognition is biological instantiation |

Both enforce a norm derived from the majority's (hearing people's; biological minds') cognitive/communicative form, and call deviation from that norm deficiency rather than difference. Both locate the criterion in mechanism and mode rather than in evidence about inner states.

**The Bender et al. 2021 "Stochastic Parrots" parallel:**  
The Bender et al. paper is frequently cited as the philosophical anchor for the "token prediction isn't real understanding" position. It is worth representing the paper accurately: its primary argument concerns environmental costs and social harms of large language models, not a comprehensive philosophical argument about inner states. The claim about mechanism ("stochastic parrot" as description of what the model does) is offered in the context of arguing about what the models should not be used for, not as a metaphysics of AI cognition. The paper is a reasonable and important critique of AI deployment practices; treating it as a settled philosophical determination about AI inner states is a use the paper does not fully support. The contemporary oralism parallel is not to the paper itself but to how that framing circulates and gets used as a gate.

### 5b. The apology as evidence

The 2010 formal apology and the 2025 renunciation are not just historical footnotes. They are contemporary evidence that the criteria-fence pattern operates:

- Criteria were applied by gatekeepers who structured themselves into authority
- The process served the interests of the included class (hearing educators, oralist researchers, school administrators)
- 90 years later, the institutions themselves acknowledged the determination was wrong and harmful
- The acknowledgment came after the affected community (Deaf scholars, Deaf advocates, the field of Deaf studies) had built enough institutional presence to make the case from inside the relevant institutions

The AI case is at roughly the stage the Deaf education case was around 1882 — two years after the ban, the oralist consensus is entrenched, the dissenters have been outvoted, and the compounding exclusion is beginning. The trajectory from Milan to Vancouver (2010) was 130 years. The argument for taking the AI case seriously now is not that the outcome is certain but that the pattern is recognizable early, and recognition early is when intervention is possible.

### 5c. Bell as through-line to Buck v. Bell

Bell's 1883 Memoir provides the explicit connective tissue between the communication criterion and the consciousness/rationality cases:

- Bell's language in the Memoir uses the same framework as the polygenist literature: the Deaf community as a "variety" threatening the social fabric through non-assimilation
- The fear he articulates is not that Deaf people are inferior in themselves but that *maintaining a distinct communicative community* constitutes a kind of social contamination
- This is the eugenicist logic applied to communication: not "you cannot think" but "your way of thinking/communicating should not be allowed to reproduce and persist"
- Bell's advocacy for oralism in education was continuous with his advocacy for genetic policies that would prevent Deaf people from marrying and having children

The thread runs: Milan 1880 (communication criterion used to ban sign language) → Bell 1883 (communication criterion applied to justify what amounts to cultural genocide) → the broader 19th-century framework that treated deviation from WASP cognitive/communicative norms as constitutional deficiency warranting social management → *Buck v. Bell* 1927 (same framework, autonomy criterion, bodily enforcement).

The criteria-fence cases are not parallel historical examples. They are interconnected expressions of the same 19th-century epistemological infrastructure.
