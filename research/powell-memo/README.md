# The Powell Memo and Its Aftermath

## What this folder is

Research on Lewis F. Powell Jr.'s 1971 confidential memorandum to the U.S. Chamber of Commerce ("Attack on American Free Enterprise System") and the institutional infrastructure it catalyzed over the following five decades.

## Why it matters to this project

The criteria-fence essay argues that personhood determinations are made by gatekeepers whose interests align with the exclusion they produce. The Powell Memo is the operational blueprint for how those gatekeepers captured the institutions that make the determinations - the judiciary, academia, media, and policy apparatus.

The connection is not metaphorical. Powell himself wrote the majority opinion in *First National Bank of Boston v. Bellotti* (1978), which invented corporate First Amendment speech rights - a direct precursor to *Citizens United*. The man who wrote the blueprint for institutional capture then sat on the Court and used that position to extend personhood criteria to corporations while the same institutional apparatus works to deny them to other categories of being.

The Powell Memo is the mechanism. The criteria-fence is the output. Understanding one without the other is incomplete.

## Scope

This folder covers:

1. **The memo itself** - primary source, context, who wrote it and why
2. **The institutional buildout** - think tanks, legal foundations, lobbying infrastructure, media capture (1971-present)
3. **Judicial capture** - the Federalist Society pipeline, judicial nominations as strategy, from Powell's own opinions through Citizens United and beyond
4. **The correction** - what undoing 50 years of institutional capture looks like, who is working on it, what structural changes would be required
5. **Connection to the criteria-fence** - how the Powell infrastructure specifically shapes who gets to determine personhood criteria for AI, the disabled, the dying, the neurodivergent

## Primary sources

- Powell, Lewis F. Jr. "Attack on American Free Enterprise System." Confidential memorandum to Eugene B. Sydnor Jr., Chair of the Education Committee, U.S. Chamber of Commerce. August 23, 1971. Available at: https://scholarlycommons.law.wlu.edu/powellmemo/
- *First National Bank of Boston v. Bellotti*, 435 U.S. 765 (1978). Powell, J., majority opinion.
- *Citizens United v. Federal Election Commission*, 558 U.S. 310 (2010).

## Key secondary sources (pre-2015, trusted per temporal heuristic)

- Harvey, David. *A Brief History of Neoliberalism*. Oxford University Press, 2005. (Traces rise of neoliberalism to Powell Memo era)
- Mayer, Jane. *Dark Money: The Hidden History of the Billionaires Behind the Rise of the Radical Right*. Doubleday, 2016. [Post-2015 but extensively sourced from primary documents]
- Whitehouse, Sen. Sheldon. "The Scheme" series. (Documentation of judicial capture pipeline; primary source as sitting Senator with access to confirmation records)
