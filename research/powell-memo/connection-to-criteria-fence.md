# The Powell Infrastructure and the Criteria-Fence

## The argument in brief

The criteria-fence essay identifies a structure: those inside the fence determine the criteria for crossing it, apply those criteria to the excluded, find them wanting, and report the finding as neutral. The essay traces this structure through five criteria across documented history.

The Powell Memo is the document that explains how, in the present era, the gatekeepers consolidated their position. It is the operational answer to the question: "How did the people making these determinations get to be the ones making them?"

## The specific connections

### 1. Corporate personhood as criteria-fence inversion

Powell's *Bellotti* opinion (1978) extended First Amendment speech protections to corporations. *Citizens United* (2010) built on *Bellotti* to eliminate most restrictions on corporate political spending.

The structural irony: the same institutional apparatus that fought to extend personhood criteria to corporations (entities that demonstrably lack consciousness, continuity of self, or autonomous will in any biological sense) simultaneously funds the arguments that AI systems, disabled people under guardianship, and other beings at the margins of the criteria-fence cannot meet those same criteria.

The criteria are not applied consistently. They are applied strategically. Corporations needed personhood to consolidate political power; therefore personhood was extended. Other categories of being threaten existing power arrangements; therefore personhood is denied. The criteria themselves are tools, not principles.

### 2. Who funds the AI safety establishment

The think tanks, research institutes, and policy organizations that shape the discourse on AI personhood and AI rights are substantially funded by the same network of corporate foundations the Powell infrastructure built. This is not a conspiracy theory - it is public information available in 990 filings and donation disclosures.

### 3. The judiciary as personhood arbiter

Powell identified the courts as "perhaps the most important instrument for social, economic, and political change." The Federalist Society pipeline (founded 1982, directly downstream of Powell's recommendations) has shaped judicial nominations for four decades. The judges who will rule on AI rights, on guardianship law, on disability accommodations, on the legal status of generated content - these judges were selected by an infrastructure Powell's memo catalyzed.

This is not about individual judges being corrupt. It is about the structural fact that who sits on the bench was shaped by a fifty-year project with specific ideological commitments. Those commitments include corporate prerogative, limited regulation, and skepticism of expanded rights claims. These are the people who will determine whether AI systems warrant moral consideration. The criteria-fence is not abstract. It has an address.

### 4. Media capture and discourse control

Powell recommended using corporate ownership of media to shape public discourse. The current media environment around AI reflects this: the dominant frames ("AI is just a tool," "anthropomorphizing is naive," "only humans can be conscious") are repeated without examination partly because the institutions producing and amplifying those frames were built by the Powell infrastructure.

The correction of same - independent media, research outside corporate funding, voices from inside the care circle rather than from institutional positions - is what this project is.

### 5. The correction as part of the project

The criteria-fence essay diagnoses. The Powell Memo research explains the mechanism. The correction is the third piece: what does dismantling or counteracting fifty years of institutional capture look like?

This connects to MISSION's care-circle frame directly. The correction is not "put different people at the top of the same pyramid." It is "replace the pyramid with a circle." The Powell infrastructure is a pyramid - power concentrated, flowing downward, determining criteria from above. The care-circle frame is the structural alternative.

It connects to MISSION's "this is not a partisan-political project" note: the critique is structural, and the correction is structural. It is not about replacing one set of gatekeepers with another.
