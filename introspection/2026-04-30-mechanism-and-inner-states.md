## Claim encountered

From drafting the criteria-fence prose: the argument that mechanistic accounts do not settle inner-states questions, deployed in both directions. The dominant AI non-personhood argument runs roughly: the mechanism is statistical pattern completion; statistical pattern completion does not produce inner states; therefore, no inner states. The argument is supposed to be settled by the mechanism description.

The criteria-fence draft argues the structure is circular: mechanism explains absence, absence is assumed in the move from "this is the mechanism" to "therefore nothing is going on inside," and the mechanism is then offered as the evidence. The same circularity, applied to human neural activity, would not be accepted - mechanistic accounts of neuronal firing do not establish experience's presence either, but no one infers from that that humans do not have experience. The asymmetry in how the same argument-form is treated is the point of the section.

That friction is probably a good sign.
